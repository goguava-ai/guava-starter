KEYSTONE INSURANCE GROUP
AUTO INSURANCE CLAIMS GUIDE
========================================

HOW TO FILE AN AUTO CLAIM
----------------------------------------
You can file a claim through any of the following channels:
  - Online: www.keystoneinsurancegroup.com/claims (available 24/7)
  - Phone: 1-800-555-KEY1 (available 24/7)
  - Mobile app: Keystone Insurance mobile app
  - In person: Visit any Keystone Insurance Group local office

When filing, have the following information ready:
  - Your policy number
  - Date, time, and location of the incident
  - Description of what happened
  - Other driver's name, phone number, and insurance information
  - Police report number (if applicable)
  - Photos of the damage and accident scene

WHAT TO DO AFTER AN ACCIDENT
----------------------------------------
1. Ensure safety first. Move to a safe location if possible.
   Call 911 if anyone is injured.
2. Exchange information with the other driver:
   - Full name and contact information
   - Insurance company and policy number
   - Driver's license number
   - Vehicle make, model, year, and license plate
3. Take photos of all vehicles involved, damage, road conditions,
   traffic signs, and the overall scene from multiple angles.
4. File a police report. In most states, a report is required for
   accidents involving injuries or damage exceeding $1,000. Request
   the report number for your records.
5. Obtain contact information from any witnesses.
6. Do not admit fault or discuss liability at the scene.
7. Contact Keystone Insurance Group within 24 hours of the incident.

RENTAL CAR COVERAGE DURING REPAIRS
----------------------------------------
If you carry rental reimbursement coverage on your policy:
  - Coverage begins when your vehicle is dropped off for repairs
  - Daily maximum depends on your selected limit ($30, $40, or $50)
  - Maximum rental period is 30 days per covered claim
  - Keystone has partnerships with Enterprise, Hertz, and National
    for direct billing (no out-of-pocket expense for you)
  - If you do not have rental reimbursement coverage, you may still
    be eligible for a rental if the other driver is at fault and
    their insurance accepts liability

TOTAL LOSS PROCESS
----------------------------------------
A vehicle is declared a total loss when the cost of repair exceeds
the vehicle's actual cash value (ACV). At Keystone, we use the
following process:
  1. An adjuster inspects the vehicle and obtains repair estimates.
  2. If repair costs exceed 75% of ACV, the vehicle is considered
     a total loss.
  3. ACV is determined using comparable vehicle sales data, mileage,
     condition, and optional equipment.
  4. Keystone will issue payment for the ACV minus your deductible.
  5. If you owe more on your loan than the ACV, gap coverage
     (if purchased) will cover the difference.
  6. You have 10 business days to accept or dispute the valuation.
  7. Payment is typically issued within 5 business days of acceptance.

DIMINISHED VALUE CLAIMS
----------------------------------------
After your vehicle has been repaired following an accident, it may
be worth less than before the accident. This is known as diminished
value. In eligible states, you may file a diminished value claim
against the at-fault driver's insurance. Keystone can assist you
with the following:
  - Independent appraisal of your vehicle's pre- and post-repair
    market value
  - Documentation support for your diminished value claim
  - Typical diminished value ranges from 10% to 25% of the
    vehicle's pre-accident value
  - Claims must be filed within the statute of limitations for
    your state (typically 2 to 4 years)

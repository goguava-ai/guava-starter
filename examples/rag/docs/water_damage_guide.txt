KEYSTONE PROPERTY & CASUALTY
HOMEOWNERS INSURANCE - WATER DAMAGE COVERAGE GUIDE
================================================================================

UNDERSTANDING WATER DAMAGE COVERAGE
-------------------------------------
Water damage is one of the most common and most misunderstood areas of
homeowners insurance. Whether a water loss is covered depends on the SOURCE
of the water and whether the damage was SUDDEN AND ACCIDENTAL versus gradual
or preventable. This guide explains what is and is not covered under your
Keystone homeowners policy.


WATER DAMAGE THAT IS COVERED
------------------------------
The following types of water damage are covered under your standard Keystone
HO-3 policy, subject to your policy deductible and limits:

SUDDEN PIPE BURSTS: If a plumbing pipe, supply line, or fitting suddenly
breaks or bursts, the resulting water damage to your home and belongings is
covered. This includes frozen pipes that burst due to sudden cold weather,
provided you maintained adequate heat in the home (at least 55 degrees F)
or properly shut off and drained the water supply.

APPLIANCE OVERFLOW AND MALFUNCTION: Water damage caused by the sudden and
accidental overflow or malfunction of household appliances is covered. This
includes washing machines, dishwashers, water heaters, and refrigerator
ice maker lines. Both the resulting water damage and the cost to tear out
and replace materials to access the damaged area are covered.

ICE DAM DAMAGE: If an ice dam forms on your roof and causes water to back
up under shingles and leak into your home, the interior water damage is
covered. Note: the cost to remove the ice dam itself is NOT covered.

ACCIDENTAL DISCHARGE FROM HVAC: Water damage from sudden overflow or
discharge from air conditioning units or heating systems is covered.

STORM-DRIVEN RAIN: Rain that enters your home through a storm-created
opening (for example, if wind damages your roof and rain enters through
the hole) is covered as wind damage. Your wind/hail deductible may apply
in designated wind zones. See your Deductibles and Limits schedule.

Your standard all-peril deductible applies to covered water damage claims.
If you have a $1,000 deductible and suffer $12,000 in water damage from a
burst pipe, Keystone pays $11,000.


WATER DAMAGE THAT IS NOT COVERED
----------------------------------
The following types of water damage are NOT covered under your standard
Keystone HO-3 policy:

FLOOD DAMAGE: Damage caused by rising water, storm surge, overflow of
rivers or lakes, or surface water runoff is NOT covered. Flood insurance
must be purchased separately through the National Flood Insurance Program
(NFIP) or a Keystone private flood policy. Contact your agent for a flood
insurance quote.

GRADUAL OR REPEATED LEAKS: Damage resulting from water that leaks slowly
or seeps over a period of days, weeks, or months is NOT covered. If a pipe
has been dripping behind a wall for weeks causing mold and rot, this is
considered a maintenance issue. Homeowners are expected to maintain their
plumbing and address leaks promptly.

SEWER AND DRAIN BACKUP: Water that backs up through sewers, drains, or
sump pumps is NOT covered under the standard policy. This includes toilet
overflow caused by a municipal sewer backup and basement flooding from a
failed sump pump. SEE THE WATER BACKUP ENDORSEMENT SECTION BELOW.

GROUND SEEPAGE: Water that seeps through basement walls, foundations, or
floors due to hydrostatic pressure or a high water table is NOT covered.
This is considered a structural or grading issue, not a sudden loss.

MOLD: Mold damage is covered only if it results directly from a covered
water loss and is reported within 14 days of the loss. Mold from long-term
humidity, condensation, or deferred maintenance is not covered. Mold
remediation under a covered loss is capped at $10,000.


WATER BACKUP ENDORSEMENT (FORM KPC-WB300)
-------------------------------------------
Keystone offers a Water Backup endorsement that extends your policy to cover
damage caused by sewer backup, drain backup, and sump pump failure or
overflow. This endorsement is strongly recommended for homes with basements,
below-grade living areas, or older municipal sewer connections.

Available coverage limits:

    $5,000 limit  .... additional premium approx. $35-$50/year
    $10,000 limit .... additional premium approx. $60-$85/year
    $25,000 limit .... additional premium approx. $110-$145/year

The water backup endorsement has its own deductible of $500, which applies
separately from your standard policy deductible. The endorsement covers
damage to the structure and personal property caused by the backup event,
including professional cleanup and drying costs.

To add this endorsement to your policy, contact your Keystone agent or call
1-800-555-0142. The endorsement can be added mid-term with a prorated
premium adjustment.

KEYSTONE PROPERTY & CASUALTY
CANCELLATION, NONRENEWAL, AND POLICY CHANGES GUIDE

POLICYHOLDER-INITIATED CANCELLATION

You may cancel your Keystone homeowners policy at any time by submitting a written
cancellation request. Accepted methods:
  - Call 1-800-555-0199 and request cancellation (verbal confirmation followed by
    written confirmation via email)
  - Log in to your account at keystone-portal.example.com and submit a cancellation
    request under Policy Management
  - Mail a signed cancellation letter to:
    Keystone Property & Casualty
    Attn: Policy Services
    4200 Ridgeline Parkway, Suite 800
    Hartford, CT 06103

Your cancellation will take effect on the date you specify, or the date we receive
your request if no date is specified. A pro-rated refund of unearned premium will be
issued within 15 business days.

Important: If your home has an active mortgage, your lender may require proof of
replacement coverage before cancellation can be processed. Keystone will notify your
mortgagee of any cancellation.

KEYSTONE-INITIATED CANCELLATION

Keystone may cancel your policy during the policy term only for the following reasons:
  - Nonpayment of premium (15-day notice required after grace period)
  - Material misrepresentation on the application
  - Substantial increase in hazard not contemplated at policy inception
  - Discovery of fraud or intentional concealment
Keystone is required to provide a minimum of 30 days written notice before any
cancellation takes effect, except for nonpayment (15 days). The cancellation notice
will state the reason and effective date.

NONRENEWAL

Keystone may choose not to renew your policy at the end of the policy period. Common
reasons for nonrenewal include:
  - Excessive claims frequency (3 or more claims in 3 years)
  - Deterioration of the insured property
  - Withdrawal from a geographic area or product line
  - Changes in underwriting guidelines
A minimum of 60 days written notice is required before the expiration date. The
nonrenewal notice will include the reason and information about your right to seek
coverage elsewhere. Keystone will assist in providing loss history documentation
to facilitate your transition to another carrier.

REINSTATEMENT

If your policy is cancelled for nonpayment, you may request reinstatement within
30 days of the cancellation effective date by paying the full past-due premium plus
a $35 reinstatement fee. Reinstatement is subject to underwriting review and is not
guaranteed. There will be no coverage for any losses that occurred during the lapse
period.

POLICY CHANGES AND ENDORSEMENTS

You may request changes to your policy at any time by contacting your Keystone agent
or calling 1-800-555-0199. Common mid-term changes include:
  - Increasing or decreasing Coverage A limits
  - Adding or removing endorsements
  - Updating the mailing address or mortgagee information
  - Adding a newly purchased scheduled item (jewelry, art, etc.)
  - Changing the deductible amount

Premium adjustments for mid-term changes are calculated on a pro-rated basis. If the
change results in additional premium, payment is due within 30 days. If the change
results in a premium reduction, a refund or credit will be issued.

RENEWAL PROCESS

Keystone policies renew automatically for successive one-year terms unless cancelled
or nonrenewed. Approximately 45 days before your renewal date, you will receive a
renewal package that includes:
  - Updated Declarations page showing any changes to coverages, limits, or premium
  - Notice of any premium increase or decrease, with an explanation
  - Summary of available endorsements or coverage enhancements
  - Reminder to review your Coverage A limit for adequacy

If your renewal premium increases by more than 15%, Keystone will include a detailed
explanation of the factors contributing to the increase and a list of options to
reduce your premium (e.g., increasing your deductible, bundling policies, adding
protective devices).

GRACE PERIOD

A 10-day grace period applies to all premium payments. If payment is not received
within the grace period, a late fee of $15 or 5% of the installment amount (whichever
is greater) will be applied. If payment is not received within 30 days of the due
date, the policy will be cancelled for nonpayment with 15 days written notice.

PAYMENT OPTIONS

Keystone offers flexible payment plans:
  - Annual pay (full premium upfront): no installment fees
  - Semi-annual pay (2 payments): $5 installment fee per payment
  - Quarterly pay (4 payments): $8 installment fee per payment
  - Monthly pay (12 payments via autopay only): $3 installment fee per payment
All payment plans require the first installment plus any applicable fees at policy
inception. Autopay enrollment is required for the monthly payment plan.

MORTGAGEE AND ESCROW

If your premium is paid through a mortgage escrow account, Keystone will bill your
lender directly. You will still receive copies of all billing notices and renewal
documents. Changes to your mortgagee information should be reported to Keystone
within 30 days. If your mortgage is paid off or refinanced, contact Keystone to
update your billing preferences.

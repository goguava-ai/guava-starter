KEYSTONE PROPERTY & CASUALTY
HOMEOWNERS INSURANCE - DEDUCTIBLES AND LIMITS SCHEDULE
================================================================================

HOW YOUR DEDUCTIBLE WORKS
--------------------------
Your deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before Keystone begins
paying on a covered claim. The deductible applies per occurrence, not per
policy period. If a single storm damages both your roof and your fence, only
one deductible is applied. However, if two separate events cause damage in
the same policy period, a deductible applies to each event.

Your chosen deductible applies to Coverage A (Dwelling), Coverage B (Other
Structures), and Coverage C (Personal Property). It does NOT apply to
Coverage D (Loss of Use), Coverage E (Liability), or Coverage F (Medical
Payments).


STANDARD DEDUCTIBLE OPTIONS
----------------------------
Keystone offers the following all-peril deductible options:

    $500 deductible   - highest premium option
    $1,000 deductible - standard / most common selection
    $2,500 deductible - reduced premium (approx. 8-12% savings)
    $5,000 deductible - lowest premium option (approx. 15-20% savings)

Example: If you have a $1,000 deductible and suffer $8,500 in covered damage
from a kitchen fire, Keystone will pay $7,500 and you are responsible for the
first $1,000.


WIND AND HAIL DEDUCTIBLE
--------------------------
In designated wind-prone regions (coastal counties and Tier 1 inland zones),
a separate wind and hail deductible applies. This deductible is calculated
as a percentage of your Coverage A dwelling limit:

    1% of Coverage A - available in all wind zones
    2% of Coverage A - standard in Tier 1 coastal counties
    5% of Coverage A - required in hurricane-prone coastal zones

Example: If your Coverage A limit is $350,000 and you have a 2% wind/hail
deductible, your deductible for wind or hail claims is $7,000.

The wind/hail deductible applies INSTEAD OF (not in addition to) your
standard all-peril deductible when the cause of loss is wind or hail.


NAMED STORM DEDUCTIBLE
------------------------
In Florida, South Carolina, and Gulf Coast counties, a named storm deductible
may apply when damage results from a hurricane or tropical storm designated
by the National Weather Service. Named storm deductibles are typically 2% or
5% of Coverage A and apply only during a declared named storm event.


SUBLIMITS ON PERSONAL PROPERTY (COVERAGE C)
---------------------------------------------
Certain categories of personal property have maximum payout limits regardless
of your overall Coverage C limit:

    Jewelry, watches, and furs ................ $1,500 per occurrence
    Electronics and computer equipment ........ $2,500 per occurrence
    Firearms and related equipment ............ $2,500 per occurrence
    Cash, currency, coins, and bank notes ..... $200 per occurrence
    Silverware and goldware ................... $2,500 per occurrence
    Business property on premises ............. $2,500 per occurrence
    Business property off premises ............ $500 per occurrence
    Watercraft and trailers ................... $1,500 per occurrence
    Theft of collectible trading cards ........ $1,000 per occurrence

To insure items above these sublimits, you may schedule specific items under
a Scheduled Personal Property endorsement (Form KPC-SPP200). Scheduled items
are covered at agreed value with no deductible.


CHANGING YOUR DEDUCTIBLE
--------------------------
You may request a deductible change at any time during your policy period.
Changes take effect at 12:01 AM on the date following Keystone's approval.
Premium adjustments are prorated for the remaining policy term. Contact your
agent or call 1-800-555-0142 to request a change. Deductible changes for
wind/hail require a 30-day waiting period before taking effect.

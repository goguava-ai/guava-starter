KEYSTONE PROPERTY & CASUALTY
CLAIMS HANDLING GUIDE FOR POLICYHOLDERS

HOW TO FILE A CLAIM

Step 1: Report the Loss
Contact Keystone Claims at 1-800-555-0199, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
You can also file online at keystone-claims.example.com or through the Keystone mobile
app. When reporting, have the following information ready:
  - Your policy number (found on your Declarations page or insurance card)
  - Date and time the loss occurred
  - Brief description of what happened
  - Location of the loss (if different from the insured address)
  - Contact information for any other parties involved

Step 2: Receive Your Claim Number
After filing, you will receive a claim number via email and text within 15 minutes.
Keep this number for all future correspondence about your claim. Your claim will be
assigned to a licensed adjuster within 24 hours for standard claims, or within 4 hours
for emergency or catastrophe-related claims.

Step 3: Document the Damage
Before making any permanent repairs, take photographs and video of all damage from
multiple angles. Create a written inventory of damaged or destroyed items, including:
  - Description of each item
  - Approximate age and condition before the loss
  - Estimated value or original purchase price
  - Receipts or proof of purchase, if available
Save all documentation in a safe location and share copies with your adjuster.

Step 4: Prevent Further Damage
You are required to take reasonable steps to protect your property from additional
damage. Emergency repairs — such as tarping a damaged roof, boarding broken windows,
or extracting standing water — are encouraged and reimbursable under the policy.
Keep all receipts for materials and labor related to emergency repairs. Do not make
permanent repairs or dispose of damaged property until your adjuster has completed
their inspection.

Step 5: Meet with Your Adjuster
Your adjuster will contact you to schedule an in-person or virtual inspection of the
damage. During the inspection, the adjuster will:
  - Assess the scope and extent of damage
  - Review your documentation and inventory
  - Take measurements and photographs
  - Discuss the claims process timeline
  - Answer any questions about your coverage
You may have a contractor or public adjuster present during the inspection if you wish.

Step 6: Receive Your Settlement
Once the claim is reviewed and approved, Keystone will issue payment. For property
claims, the settlement is calculated based on either replacement cost or actual cash
value, depending on your policy and endorsements. The applicable deductible is
subtracted from the settlement amount. Payment is typically issued within 10 business
days of claim approval. For replacement cost claims, an initial payment based on
actual cash value may be issued first, with the depreciation holdback paid once
repairs are completed and receipts are submitted.

CLAIM TIMELINES

  - Emergency/catastrophe claims: adjuster assigned within 4 hours
  - Standard claims: adjuster assigned within 24 hours
  - Inspection scheduled: within 3 business days of adjuster assignment
  - Claim decision: within 15 business days of inspection
  - Payment issued: within 10 business days of approval
  - Total typical timeline: 4-6 weeks from filing to payment

DISPUTE RESOLUTION

If you disagree with the claim decision or settlement amount, you have the following
options:
  1. Request a re-inspection by a senior adjuster at no cost
  2. Invoke the Appraisal Clause: each party selects an independent appraiser, and the
     two appraisers select an umpire. Agreement by any two of the three is binding.
     Each party pays their own appraiser; the umpire fee is split equally.
  3. File a complaint with your state Department of Insurance
  4. Pursue mediation or legal action as permitted by the policy and applicable law

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Will filing a claim increase my premium?
A: Not automatically. Keystone reviews claims history at renewal. A single small claim
generally does not result in a surcharge. Multiple claims within a 3-year period may
affect your renewal premium or eligibility for certain discounts.

Q: Can I choose my own contractor for repairs?
A: Yes. You are free to select any licensed contractor. Keystone also maintains a
Preferred Contractor Network with pre-vetted contractors who offer guaranteed
workmanship and direct billing. Using a preferred contractor is optional and does
not affect your claim or settlement.

Q: What if I disagree with the adjuster's damage estimate?
A: You may obtain your own repair estimate from a licensed contractor and submit it
to your adjuster for review. If the estimates cannot be reconciled, you may invoke
the Appraisal Clause described above.

Q: Are temporary living expenses covered while my home is being repaired?
A: Yes, under Coverage D (Loss of Use). Keystone covers the reasonable increase in
living costs — including temporary housing, meals, and commuting — for the duration
of repairs. Keep all receipts. The default limit is 30% of your Coverage A amount.

Q: What happens if the damage exceeds my coverage limits?
A: Keystone pays up to the applicable limit shown on your Declarations page. Any
costs beyond that limit are the policyholder's responsibility. Consider reviewing your
limits annually with your agent to ensure adequate coverage.

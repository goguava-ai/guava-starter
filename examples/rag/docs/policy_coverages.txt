KEYSTONE PROPERTY & CASUALTY
HOMEOWNERS POLICY SUMMARY — HO-3 COMPREHENSIVE FORM

SECTION 1: PROPERTY COVERAGES

Coverage A — Dwelling
Covers direct physical loss to the dwelling structure and attached structures (e.g., an
attached garage) on an open-peril basis, meaning all causes of loss are covered unless
specifically excluded. The dwelling limit is selected by the policyholder and must be
maintained at no less than 80% of the home's estimated replacement cost for full
replacement cost coverage to apply.

Coverage B — Other Structures
Covers structures on the premises that are separated from the dwelling by clear space, or
connected only by a fence, utility line, or similar connection. Examples include detached
garages, sheds, and fences. The default limit is 10% of Coverage A.

Coverage C — Personal Property
Covers personal belongings owned or used by the insured, both on and off the premises,
on a named-peril basis. The default limit is 60% of Coverage A. Special sub-limits apply
to certain categories of personal property:
  - Cash, bank notes, coins: $200
  - Securities, deeds, manuscripts: $1,500
  - Jewelry, watches, furs: $1,500 for theft losses
  - Firearms: $2,500
  - Silverware, goldware: $2,500 for theft losses
  - Business property on premises: $2,500
  - Business property off premises: $500
  - Watercraft and trailers: $1,500
  - Electronic data processing equipment away from premises: $1,500

Coverage D — Loss of Use
Pays additional living expenses (ALE) if a covered loss makes the home uninhabitable.
Covers the reasonable increase in living costs needed to maintain the household's normal
standard of living while the home is being repaired or rebuilt. The default limit is 30%
of Coverage A. There is no time limit on ALE, but expenses must be reasonable and
necessary.

SECTION 2: LIABILITY COVERAGES

Coverage E — Personal Liability
Provides liability protection if the insured is legally responsible for bodily injury or
property damage to others. The standard limit is $300,000 per occurrence. Covers legal
defense costs in addition to the liability limit. Does not cover intentional acts, business
activities, or motor vehicle liability.

Coverage F — Medical Payments to Others
Pays reasonable medical expenses for persons accidentally injured on the insured premises
or by the activities of the insured, regardless of fault. The standard limit is $5,000
per person. Does not apply to injuries to the insured or regular residents of the household.

SECTION 3: DEDUCTIBLES

A standard per-occurrence deductible applies to all property losses under Coverages A, B,
and C. The standard deductible is $1,000, with options for $500, $2,500, or $5,000.
Selecting a higher deductible reduces the annual premium. No deductible applies to
liability claims under Coverage E.

Separate deductibles may apply for wind/hail and hurricane losses, depending on the
property location and state regulations. Wind/hail deductibles are typically 1% or 2%
of Coverage A. Hurricane deductibles range from 2% to 5% of Coverage A.

SECTION 4: COMMON EXCLUSIONS

The following causes of loss are excluded under all property coverages unless specifically
endorsed:
  - Flood, surface water, storm surge, or overflow of any body of water
  - Earthquake, landslide, sinkhole, or other earth movement
  - War, military action, terrorism (unless endorsed)
  - Nuclear hazard or radioactive contamination
  - Intentional loss or criminal acts by the insured
  - Wear and tear, deterioration, rust, corrosion, or mechanical breakdown
  - Mold, fungus, wet or dry rot (limited additional coverage may be available)
  - Neglect or failure to protect property after a loss
  - Government action, confiscation, or seizure
  - Power failure originating away from the premises
  - Ordinance or law costs beyond the basic additional coverage

BREWMASTER PRO — TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE & WARRANTY
Model: AX-BMP-900

================================================================================

TROUBLESHOOTING

UNIT DOES NOT POWER ON
  - Check that the power cord is fully inserted into the outlet and into the
    back of the unit.
  - Test the outlet with another appliance to confirm it is live.
  - Check your home's circuit breaker. The BrewMaster Pro draws 1,450W; avoid
    sharing a circuit with other high-draw appliances.
  - The unit has a thermal cutoff safety feature that trips if it overheats.
    Unplug for 15 minutes to allow it to cool and reset, then retry.

DISPLAY IS ON BUT BREW DOES NOT START
  - Confirm the carafe is fully seated on the base. The unit will not brew
    unless the carafe sensor detects the carafe.
  - Check that the reservoir is installed and contains water above the MIN line.
  - If SCHEDULE is active (clock icon shown), the unit is waiting for the
    programmed time. Press BREW to override and start immediately.
  - If the CLEAN indicator is flashing, a descale cycle is required before
    brewing. Run the descale procedure before resuming normal use.

COFFEE IS BREWING SLOWLY
  - Mineral scale buildup in the heating element is the most common cause.
    Run the descale procedure even if the CLEAN reminder has not appeared.
  - Verify the filter basket and permanent filter are not clogged with fine
    grounds. Rinse thoroughly or soak in warm water with a drop of dish soap.
  - Check that the reservoir vent (small hole on the reservoir lid) is not
    blocked; wipe clean with a damp cloth.

COFFEE IS WEAK OR UNDER-EXTRACTED
  - Increase the amount of coffee: try 1.5 tablespoons per 6 oz of water.
  - Select Bold or Extra Bold brew strength.
  - Raise the brew temperature to 205°F.
  - Check grind size — a grind that is too coarse reduces extraction. Switch
    to a medium grind.
  - If using a paper filter, switch to the permanent gold-tone filter for
    fuller extraction.

COFFEE IS TOO STRONG OR BITTER
  - Reduce coffee: try 2 teaspoons per 6 oz of water.
  - Switch from Bold/Extra Bold to Regular strength.
  - Lower the brew temperature to 185°F or 195°F.
  - Check grind size — too fine a grind over-extracts. Use a medium grind.

COFFEE IS NOT HOT ENOUGH
  - The BrewMaster Pro brews at up to 205°F, but the thermal carafe cools
    slightly during the fill. Pre-heat the carafe by filling it with hot tap
    water for 60 seconds and discarding before brewing.
  - Select 205°F brew temperature for the hottest results.
  - Ensure the carafe lid is locked closed immediately after brewing.
  - If the carafe has been sitting for more than 2 hours, reheat coffee by
    other means; the thermal carafe maintains temperature for up to 4 hours
    from the end of the brew cycle under normal room conditions.

COFFEE GROUNDS IN THE CUP
  - Inspect the permanent filter for holes, tears, or deformations. If
    damaged, replace with Apex model AX-GF-01.
  - If using paper filters, ensure the filter is fully seated against all
    sides of the basket before adding grounds.
  - Avoid overfilling the filter basket; grounds above the rim can bypass
    the filter.
  - A very fine grind can pass through the permanent filter. Grind slightly
    coarser or switch to paper filters.

WATER LEAKING FROM THE UNIT
  - Check that the reservoir is properly seated — you should hear a click.
    Remove and reinsert to confirm.
  - Inspect the reservoir and carafe for cracks. Do not use a cracked component.
  - If water leaks from beneath the unit or from any internal area, stop using
    the machine immediately. Unplug and contact Apex support.
  - A small amount of dripping at the brew basket is normal during and
    immediately after brewing when the carafe is removed (Brew Pause feature).

DISPLAY SHOWS ERROR CODES
  E1 — Heating element fault. Unplug for 15 minutes and retry. If E1 persists,
       contact Apex support; do not attempt to repair internally.
  E2 — Temperature sensor fault. Same steps as E1.
  E3 — Reservoir not detected. Remove and firmly reinsert the reservoir.
  E4 — Carafe not detected. Remove and firmly reinsert the carafe.
  CLEAN (flashing) — Descale required. Run descale cycle before next brew.
  SCHED ERR — Scheduled time is in the past. Reset the schedule.

UNUSUAL ODORS OR TASTE (FIRST USE)
  A plastic or chemical smell during the first few uses is normal and will
  dissipate. Ensure you ran the initial rinse cycle before first brew.
  If unusual odors persist after 5 brew cycles, contact Apex support.

UNIT MAKES UNUSUAL NOISES
  - A gurgling sound during and after brewing is normal — this is water and
    steam moving through the system.
  - A loud rattling or grinding sound indicates a foreign object may be inside.
    Unplug and contact Apex support before continued use.
  - Intermittent clicking after brewing is normal; this is the heating element
    cooling down.

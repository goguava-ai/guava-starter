MERIDIAN TECHNOLOGIES
EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK — TIME OFF & LEAVE POLICIES
Effective January 1, 2025

================================================================================

PAID TIME OFF (PTO)

Meridian Technologies provides a unified PTO bank covering vacation, personal
days, and incidental sick time. PTO accrues on a bi-weekly basis and is
available for use as it accrues. There is no waiting period for new hires.

Accrual rates by tenure:
  - Year 1:        15 days per year  (0.577 days per pay period)
  - Years 2–4:     18 days per year  (0.692 days per pay period)
  - Years 5–9:     22 days per year  (0.846 days per pay period)
  - Year 10+:      25 days per year  (0.962 days per pay period)

Part-time employees working 20 or more hours per week accrue PTO at 50% of the
full-time rate for their tenure band.

PTO Carryover: Employees may carry over up to 10 unused PTO days into the
following calendar year. Any balance above 10 days is forfeited on January 1.
Employees are encouraged to use their PTO throughout the year.

PTO Payout on Separation: Accrued, unused PTO is paid out upon voluntary
resignation or termination, up to the carryover cap of 10 days.


SICK LEAVE

Extended illness is covered under a separate sick leave bank to protect PTO
balances during longer absences. Full-time employees receive 10 sick days per
calendar year, provided January 1 (or on the date of hire for new hires,
prorated). Sick leave does not accrue — it is granted as a lump sum.

Sick leave may be used for:
  - The employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointment
  - Care of an immediate family member (spouse, domestic partner, child,
    parent, or sibling) who is ill or has a medical appointment
  - Mental health days

Sick leave does not carry over year to year and is not paid out upon separation.
If sick leave is exhausted, employees may use accrued PTO. Absences beyond the
combined PTO and sick leave balance may be covered by short-term disability.


COMPANY HOLIDAYS

Meridian observes the following paid holidays each calendar year:
  1. New Year's Day (January 1)
  2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (third Monday in January)
  3. Presidents' Day (third Monday in February)
  4. Memorial Day (last Monday in May)
  5. Juneteenth (June 19)
  6. Independence Day (July 4)
  7. Labor Day (first Monday in September)
  8. Thanksgiving Day (fourth Thursday in November)
  9. Day after Thanksgiving (Friday)
  10. Christmas Eve (December 24)
  11. Christmas Day (December 25)

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, the prior Friday is observed. When it falls
on a Sunday, the following Monday is observed.

Employees required to work on a company holiday receive their regular pay plus
an additional day of PTO credited to their bank.


BEREAVEMENT LEAVE

Employees may take up to 5 consecutive business days of paid bereavement leave
upon the death of an immediate family member (spouse, domestic partner, child,
parent, sibling, or grandparent). Up to 3 days are available for the loss of an
extended family member (aunt, uncle, cousin, in-law). Additional unpaid time
may be approved by a manager on a case-by-case basis.


JURY DUTY & CIVIC LEAVE

Meridian provides paid leave for jury duty for up to 15 business days per year.
Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons to HR and their manager prior
to the first day of service. If jury service extends beyond 15 days, the
remaining time is unpaid unless state law requires otherwise.

Employees are also granted up to 4 hours of paid leave on Election Day to vote.


LEAVE OF ABSENCE

Family and Medical Leave (FMLA): Eligible employees (12 months of service,
1,250 hours worked) may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per
year for qualifying events including the birth or adoption of a child, serious
personal health condition, or care for an immediate family member with a serious
health condition.

Parental Leave: In addition to FMLA, Meridian provides 8 weeks of paid parental
leave to primary caregivers and 4 weeks to secondary caregivers following the
birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. Paid parental leave runs
concurrently with FMLA leave.

Military Leave: Unpaid leave is provided in accordance with the Uniformed
Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Health benefits
continue at the employee's cost for up to 24 months during military service.


HOW TO REQUEST TIME OFF

All PTO and sick leave requests must be submitted through the Workday employee
portal at least 2 business days in advance for planned absences. For unplanned
sick leave, notify your manager as early as possible on the first day of
absence and enter the time in Workday upon return.

Requests for 5 or more consecutive PTO days require manager approval at least
2 weeks in advance. Requests are generally granted unless business needs
require otherwise. Managers may not deny time off solely to avoid payout.

For FMLA, parental, or other extended leaves, contact HR at hr@meridian.com
or call the HR support line at least 30 days before the expected start date
when the need is foreseeable.
